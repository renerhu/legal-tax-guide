10 Tax Deductions Every Small Business Owner Should Know in 2026

📅 May 27, 2026 • 📖 8 min read • 🏷️ Save Money

Tax deductions are how the government encourages business activity. They're not loopholes — they're intentional incentives. Yet most small business owners leave thousands of dollars on the table every year.

Here are the top 10 deductions you need to know for 2026.

1. Home Office Deduction

If you use a portion of your home exclusively and regularly for business, you can deduct $5 per square foot (up to 300 sq ft, max $1,500) using the simplified method, or actual expenses (mortgage interest, utilities, repairs, insurance).

2. Section 179 Equipment Deduction

Section 179 lets you deduct the full purchase price of qualifying equipment in the year you buy it — instead of depreciating over years. Computers, furniture, software, vehicles, and machinery all qualify. 2026 limit: up to $1.22 million.

3. Vehicle & Mileage Deduction

You can deduct actual vehicle expenses OR use the standard mileage rate (67¢/mile in 2026). If you drive 10,000 business miles, that's $6,700. Keep a mileage log! Apps like Stride or MileIQ make this automatic.

4. Health Insurance Premiums

If you're self-employed and pay for your own health insurance, you can deduct 100% of premiums for yourself, your spouse, and your dependents — directly on Form 1040. This reduces your AGI and your self-employment tax.

5. Retirement Contributions (SEP IRA)

Self-employed? Open a SEP IRA. You can contribute up to 25% of your net earnings (max $69,000 in 2026). The contribution is tax-deductible and grows tax-deferred. Best way to reduce your tax bill while building long-term wealth.

6. Software & Subscriptions

Every SaaS tool you use for business is deductible. ChatGPT, Canva, QuickBooks, project management tools, domain names, web hosting, email marketing — all 100% deductible. Keep your subscription receipts organized.

7. Continuing Education

Courses, certifications, conferences, books, and online learning materials that maintain or improve skills for your current business are fully deductible. Did you buy a course on AI tools? Deduct it. Attend a conference? Flights, hotel, and registration are deductible.

8. Business Meals (50%)

Meals with clients, prospects, or business partners are 50% deductible. The meal must be directly related to business — not just lunch with friends. Keep receipts with the business purpose and who you met with.

9. Internet & Phone

Deduct the business-use percentage of your internet and phone bills. If you use your phone 60% for business, deduct 60% of the bill. For your primary cell phone, the IRS generally allows the full cost if it's your only business line.

10. Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction

This is one of the most powerful deductions. Most pass-through business owners (sole props, LLCs, S-Corps) can deduct up to 20% of their qualified business income. For a freelancer earning $100,000, that's a $20,000 deduction. Income limits apply ($191,950 single / $383,900 married filing jointly in 2026).

⚠️ Important: Deduction rules can be complex. Always consult with a licensed CPA to ensure you qualify and are filing correctly. This guide is educational, not professional advice.
